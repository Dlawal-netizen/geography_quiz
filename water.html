IGCSE Geography: Water Notes

1. Methods of Water Supply

Reservoirs and Dams

Large barriers built across rivers store water in artificial lakes.

  • Examples: Three Gorges Dam (China), Aswan High Dam (Egypt).
  • Advantages: Provides consistent water supply, generates hydroelectric power, and prevents floods.
  • Disadvantages: Expensive, displaces communities, affects ecosystems.

Wells and Boreholes

Water is extracted from underground aquifers.

  • Examples: Used in rural areas of India, Africa, and the Middle East.
  • Advantages: Cheap, reduces reliance on seasonal rainfall.
  • Disadvantages: Over-extraction leads to depletion, contamination risks.

3. Causes of Water Shortages

Physical Causes

  • Low rainfall: the water demand is higher than the supply through rain
  • High temperatures: this causes evaporation to exceed precipitation, more water molecules held as vapor
  • More use of groundwater: due to higher demand for water and water is less percolating to replenish the underground
  • glaciers: Climate change causes glaciers to melt too quickly, leading to short-term flooding but long-term water shortages.

Human Causes

  • Overpopulation:More people means higher demand for drinking water, sanitation, and agriculture. Water sources like rivers and lakes are overused, leading to depletion.
  • Pollution : Industrial waste, agricultural runoff (pesticides, fertilizers), and sewage contamination make water unsafe to use.
  • Deforestation: Trees help regulate rainfall and groundwater recharge by absorbing and slowly releasing water.Deforestation reduces rainfall (fewer trees means less transpiration and moisture in the air). Soil erosion increases, making water sources muddy and reducing water storage capacity.
  • Inefficient irrigation: Traditional flood irrigation wastes a lot of water, as most evaporates or runs off.Over-irrigation drains groundwater faster than it can be replenished. In LEDCs, leaky pipes and outdated systems lead to massive water losses.

4. Impact of Water Shortages

Health risks: Waterborne diseases like cholera.

Time consumption: Collecting water reduces time for education and work.

Economic impact: Limits industrial growth, reduces agriculture, and increases government spending.

5. Sustainable Water Management

  • Water conservation (drip irrigation, efficient usage)
  • Recycling and reusing water (greywater recycling)
  • International agreements (Nile River Treaty)
  • Improving infrastructure (reservoirs, rainwater harvesting)

6. Case Study: Three Gorges Dam (China)

Why built? Control floods, store water, generate electricity.

Positive impacts: Supplies water, prevents flooding, provides hydroelectric power.

Negative impacts: 1.3 million displaced, ecosystem damage, high maintenance costs.
