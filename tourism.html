– IGCSE GEOGRAPHY (2025-2027 SYLLABUS)

1. Definition of Tourism

Tourism refers to the activities of people traveling to and staying in places outside their usual environment for leisure, business, or other purposes for more than 24 hours but less than a year.

2. Growth of Tourism

Factors Encouraging Growth

  • Improved Transport – Air travel, rail networks, and highways have made travel faster and more affordable.
  • Increased Disposable Income – More people can afford holidays.
  • Longer Paid Holidays – Employees have more time to travel.
  • Media and Advertising – Exposure to travel destinations through TV, social media, and online platforms.
  • Globalization – Increased international business and cultural exchanges.
  • Political Stability – Safe travel destinations attract more tourists.
  • Technological Advances – Online booking, virtual tours, and digital travel guides ease planning.

3. Types of Tourism

  • Mass Tourism – Large numbers of people visiting a destination.
  • Ecotourism – Sustainable tourism focused on conserving the environment.
  • Cultural Tourism – Visiting places of historical or cultural significance.
  • Adventure Tourism – Activities such as trekking, rafting, and mountaineering.
  • Medical Tourism – Traveling for healthcare and medical treatment.
  • Religious Tourism – Pilgrimage to religious sites.
  • Business Tourism – Attending conferences, meetings, or trade fairs.

4. Case Study: A Tourist Area or Resort

A specific case study is required where students analyze:

  • Reasons for its growth as a tourist destination
  • Impacts of tourism (economic, environmental, social)
  • Management strategies for sustainable tourism

5. Positive and Negative Impacts of Tourism

Economic Impacts

Positive:

  • Creates employment opportunities.
  • Generates foreign exchange and boosts GDP.
  • Encourages infrastructure development.

Negative:

  • Seasonal employment can lead to job insecurity.
  • Over-dependence on tourism makes economies vulnerable.
  • Increased living costs for locals due to tourism-driven inflation.

Environmental Impacts

Positive:

  • Encourages conservation and establishment of national parks.
  • Promotes awareness of environmental issues.

Negative:

  • Pollution from transport and waste.
  • Destruction of natural habitats.
  • Overcrowding leading to resource depletion.

Social and Cultural Impacts

Positive:

  • Encourages cultural exchange and preservation.
  • Enhances the quality of local facilities and services.

Negative:

  • Can lead to cultural erosion and loss of traditions.
  • Disrupts local communities with overcrowding and noise.
  • Increased crime rates in tourist-heavy areas.

6. Sustainable Tourism

Strategies for Sustainable Tourism

  • Eco-friendly accommodation – Use of renewable energy and waste reduction.
  • Local community involvement – Ensuring that locals benefit financially and culturally.
  • Legislation and policies – Governments implementing limits on tourism activities.
  • Promotion of responsible tourism – Encouraging tourists to respect local customs and environments.

7. The Role of Governments and Organizations in Managing Tourism

  • Government policies – Setting regulations, investing in infrastructure, and promoting destinations.
  • UNESCO and World Tourism Organization (WTO) – Promoting heritage conservation and sustainable tourism.
  • Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) – Working on conservation and community tourism projects.

8. Future Trends in Tourism

  • Growth of domestic tourism – Post-pandemic preference for local travel.
  • Technology-driven tourism – Virtual reality experiences and AI-assisted travel planning.
  • Green and responsible travel – Rising demand for eco-tourism and carbon-neutral trips.
  • Space Tourism – The emergence of commercial space travel as an industry.

Conclusion

Tourism plays a crucial role in global economies, but it must be managed responsibly to ensure long-term benefits. Sustainable tourism strategies can help preserve cultural heritage, protect the environment, and improve the quality of life for local communities while allowing the industry to thrive.
