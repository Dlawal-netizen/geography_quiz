Coastal Processes

Introduction

Coastal processes play a significant role in shaping the world's coastlines, influencing the formation of various landforms and the dynamic interactions between land and sea. These processes can be divided into erosion, transportation, and deposition, all of which are driven by wave action, tidal movements, and currents.

1. Wave Action

Waves are the primary agents of coastal processes and are generated by wind blowing across the surface of the sea. The energy of a wave depends on:

  • Wind speed
  • Duration of wind
  • Fetch: The distance over which the wind blows across open water

There are two main types of waves:

  • Constructive Waves: Low-energy waves with a strong swash and weak backwash, building up beaches.
  • Destructive Waves: High-energy waves with a strong backwash and weak swash, removing material from the coast.

2. Coastal Erosion

Erosion is the wearing away of the coastline by wave action, currents, and tides. The key processes of coastal erosion are:

  • Hydraulic Action: The force of waves compresses air in cracks, weakening and breaking the rock.
  • Abrasion: Rocks carried by waves scrape and wear away the cliff face.
  • Attrition: Rocks and pebbles collide, breaking into smaller, smoother pieces.
  • Solution: Weak acids in seawater dissolve soluble minerals like limestone.

3. Coastal Transportation

Transportation involves the movement of material along the coastline, influenced by wave action. Processes include:

  • Traction: Large boulders and rocks roll along the seabed.
  • Saltation: Small pebbles and stones bounce along the seabed.
  • Suspension: Fine particles, such as silt, are carried within the water column.
  • Solution: Dissolved minerals are transported invisibly in the water.

Longshore Drift: Material moves in a zigzag motion along the coast due to angled wave approaches.

4. Coastal Deposition

Deposition occurs when waves lose energy and deposit material. Influencing factors include:

  • Decrease in wave energy
  • Presence of obstacles, such as vegetation or groynes
  • Supply of sediment from rivers or eroded cliffs

Depositional landforms include beaches, spits, bars, and tombolos.

5. Coastal Landforms

Coastal landforms are categorized into:

  • Erosional Landforms: Cliffs, wave-cut platforms, caves, arches, stacks, and stumps.
  • Depositional Landforms: Beaches, spits, bars, and tombolos.

6. Coastal Management

Coastal management involves strategies to protect coastlines:

  • Hard Engineering: Sea walls, groynes, rock armour, and gabions.
  • Soft Engineering: Beach nourishment, managed retreat, and dune regeneration.
